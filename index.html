Burnout is a real thing in college, and it's taking an increasingly serious toll on students. Between exams, work, and personal responsibilities, it’s easy to lose your motivation and feel emotionally drained.

Burnout is more than tiredness—it’s a state of physical and emotional exhaustion caused by prolonged stress. The academic system often rewards overwork, making students push themselves to the brink. But creating boundaries and asking for help is a strength, not a weakness.

Strategies to Improve

  • Take Your Time: Try the Pomodoro method: 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes rest.
  • Take Care of Your Mind: Avoid doomscrolling before bed—it keeps your brain in alert mode.
  • Take Care of Your Body: Move for joy: a walk, stretch, or brief dance break. Eat real food and stay hydrated.
  • Establish Connection: Talk to trusted friends or mentors when overwhelmed.
